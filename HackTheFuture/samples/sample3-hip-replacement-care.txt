HIP PRECAUTIONS - POSTERIOR APPROACH
*** FOLLOW FOR 12 WEEKS TO PREVENT HIP DISLOCATION ***

1. DO NOT bend hip past 90 degrees
   - Use raised toilet seat (provided)
   - Use hip kit for dressing (sock aid, long-handled shoe horn, reacher)
   - Do not lean forward while sitting
   - Do not pick items up from floor

2. DO NOT cross legs or ankles
   - Keep legs separated at all times
   - Use abduction pillow when sleeping on back
   - Place pillow between legs when side-lying (lie on non-operative side only)

3. DO NOT rotate operated leg inward
   - Keep toes pointing forward or slightly outward
   - Do not pivot on operated leg

WEIGHT BEARING STATUS:
- Weight bearing as tolerated (WBAT) with walker or crutches
- Use assistive device for minimum 4 weeks or until cleared by surgeon
- Progress from walker to cane as strength improves

WOUND CARE:
- Surgical incision is closed with absorbable sutures and surgical glue
- Keep incision clean and dry
- No soaking in tub, pool, or hot tub for 4 weeks
- May shower 48 hours after surgery - let water run over incision, pat dry
- No creams, lotions, or ointments on incision

SIGNS OF WOUND INFECTION - Contact office immediately:
- Increasing redness around incision
- Drainage that is cloudy, yellow, or foul-smelling
- Fever >101.5°F
- Increasing pain not controlled by medication
- Wound opening

PHYSICAL THERAPY:
- Home health physical therapy will begin within 3-5 days of discharge
- Agency: Healing Hands Home Health - they will contact you
- Therapy 3 times per week for approximately 4 weeks
- Then transition to outpatient physical therapy
- Exercises to perform daily:
  * Ankle pumps (20 reps every hour while awake)
  * Quad sets (10 reps, 3 times daily)
  * Gluteal squeezes (10 reps, 3 times daily)
  * Heel slides (10 reps, 3 times daily)

ACTIVITY GUIDELINES:

Weeks 1-2:
- Rest and ice as needed (20 minutes on, 20 minutes off)
- Walk with walker 3-4 times daily in home
- Perform exercises as instructed
- Sleep on back or non-operative side with pillow between legs

Weeks 3-6:
- Gradually increase walking distance
- May use cane when feeling stable
- Begin outpatient physical therapy
- May ride in car (for appointments) after 2 weeks

Weeks 6-12:
- Continue hip precautions
- Increase activity as tolerated
- Most patients can drive automatic transmission at 6 weeks (if off opioids)

After 12 weeks:
- Hip precautions typically lifted
- Return to most normal activities
- No high-impact sports (running, jumping)
- Golf, swimming, cycling typically permitted
